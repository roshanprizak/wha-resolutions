ASSESSMENT  OF THE SOLOMON  ISLANDS 
The Thirty-sixth  World Health Assembly, 
Noting that the Solomon  Islands,  a Member of the United Nations,  became a 
Member of the World Health Organization  by depositing  with the Secretary-General  of 
the United Nations  a formal instrument  of acceptance  of the WHO Constitution  on 
4 April 1983; 
Noting that the United Nations  General  Assembly，  in resolution  37/l25, 
established  the assessment  of the Solomon  Islands  at the rate of 0.01% for the years 
1983 to 1985; 
Recalling  the principle  established  in resolution  WHA8.5,  and confirmed  in 
resolution  WHA24.12,  that the latest available  United Nations  scale of assessments 
should be used as a basis for determining  the scale of assessments  to be used by WHO; 
Recalling  further  that the Twenty-sixth  World Health Assembly,  in resolution 
WHA26.21,  affirmed  its belief that the scale of assessments  in WHO should follow as 
closely as possible  that of the United Nations;

to one-third  of 0.01%.

e The Twenty-ninth World Health Assembly, Having considered the valuable contribution of international organizations to the unification of standards for measurement in all branches of science Recognizing the importance of uniform standards and terminology for scientific communication and the international exchange of information; Welcoming the current move to greater uniformity of standards and units of measurement throughout the world Mindful nevertheless of the difficulties that might arise through too precipitate introduction into medical practice of certain units of the Systme international d'Units (SI), such as the substitution of the pascal for the millimetre of mercury in the measurement of blood pressure REQUESTS the Director-General : (1) to study this matter, and the possible effects of the proposed changes on the international exchange of health information, together with other international organizations as maybe appropriate; (2) to report thereon to the Thirtieth World Health Assembly. Handb. Res., Vol. II Thirteenth plenary meeting, 20 May 1976 (Committee A, fifth report)

16 May 1980 
ASSESSMENT  OF NEW MEMBERS  AND ASSOCIATE  MEMBERS: 
ASSESSMENT  OF THE SEYCHELLES 
The Thirty-third  World Health Assembly, 
Noting that the Seychelles,  a Member of 
World Health Organization  by depositing  with 
a formal instrument  of acceptance  of the WHO the United Nations,  became a Member of the 
the Secretary-General  of the United Nations 
Constitution  on 11 September  1979; 
Noting that the United Nations  General  Assembly,  in resolutions  32/39 and 34/6， 
established  the assessment  of the Seychelles  at the rate of 0.01% for the years 1978 to 1982; 
Recalling  the principle  established  in resolution  WHA8.5,  and confirmed  in resolution 
WHA24.12,  that the latest available  United Nations  scale of assessments  should be used as a 
basis for determining  the scale of assessments  to be used by WHO; 
Recalling  further  that the Twenty-sixth  World Health Assembly,  in resolution  WHA26 .21, 
affirmed  its belief that the scale of assessments  in WHO should follow as closely  as possible 
that of the United Nations; 
DECIDES 
(1) that the Seychelles  shall be assessed  at the rate of 0.01% for 1979 and future 
financial  periods; 
(2) that the assessment  for 1979 shall be reduced  to one-ninth  of 0.01%. 
Fourteenth 
A33/VR/14 plenary meeting,  16 May 1980

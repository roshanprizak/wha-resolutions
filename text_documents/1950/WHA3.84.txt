WHA3.84  Action  Taken  by Certain  Countries  with  respect  to Membership  of WHO 
The Third  World  Health  Assembly, 
Having  noted  the communications  received  from  the Vice-Minister  of Health  of the Union  of Soviet Socialist  Republics,122  the  Vice-Minister  of Health  of the Ukrainian  Soviet  Socialist  Republic,  the ViceMinister  of Health  of the Byelorussian  Soviet  Socialist  Republic,  the Minister  for Foreign  Affairs  of the People's  Republic  of Bulgaria,123  the  Minister  of Health  of the People's  Republic  of Roumania,124  the Minister  for Foreign  Affairs  of the People's  Republic  of Albania,124  and  the Minister  for Foreign  Affairs 
of the Republic  of Czechoslovakia,124  notifying  the Director-General  that  their  States  no longer  consider 
themselves  Members  of the World  Health  Organization, 
RESOLVES  that  while  the World  Health  Organization  will  always  welcome  the resumption  by these 
Members  of full  co-operation  in the work  of the Organization,  it is not considered  that  any  further  action 
at this  stage  is desirable. (Fifth  report  of the  Committee  on  Administration, Finance  and  Legal  Matters,  adopted  at  sixth plenary  meeting,  19 May  1950)  [A3/R/40]

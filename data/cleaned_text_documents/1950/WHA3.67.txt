The Third World Health Assembly 1. APPROVES the programme for nursing as contained in the Proposed Programme and Budget Estimates for 1951 ; 2. ACCEPTS the views of the Expert Committee on Nursing that developing health programmes call for measures to increase and improve the supply of nurses of all types and for better use of the supply ; 3. EMPHASIZES that programmes of nursing education should be so planned that all nurses are given an understanding of the social and preventive aspects of modern health work, and 4. REQUESTS the Executive Board and the Director-General to take into consideration the views expressed on this subject by the Committee on Programme when implementing the programme.80 (Seventh report of the Committee on Programme, adopted at seventh plenarv meeting, 25 May 1950) [A3 / R / 123]

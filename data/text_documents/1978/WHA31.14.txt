WHA31.14  Assessment  of Djibouti 
The Thirty-first  World  Health  Assembly, 
Noting  that  Djibouti,  a Member  of the  United  Nations,  became  a Member  of the  World  Health 
Organization  by depositing  with  the  Secretary-General  of the  United  Nations  a formal  instrument  of 
acceptance  of the WHO  Constitution  on 10 March  1978; 
Recalling  that  the Twenty-second  World  Health  Assembly,  in resolution  WHA22.6,  decided  that  from 
1968  new  Members  shall  be assessed  in accordance  with  the practice  followed  by the United  Nations  in assessing new  Members  for their  year  of admission; 
DECIDES： 
(1) that  Djibouti  shall  be assessed  for 1978  and  future  years  at a rate  to be fixed  by the Health  Assembly, 
as and  when  the assessment  rate  for this  country  has  been  established  by the General  Assembly  of the 
United  Nations  ； 
(2) that  Djibouti  shall  be assessed  at the provisional  rates  of 0.02%  for 1978  and  0.01  % for 1979  and 
future  years,  to be adjusted  to the definitive  assessment  rate  when  established  by the Health  Assembly; 
(3) that  the assessment  for 1978  shall  be reduced  to one-third  of 0.02  %. 
Hbk Res.,  Vol.  II (2nd  ed.)，7.1.2.2  Tenth  plenary  meeting,  18 May  1978 
(Committee  B,  second  report)

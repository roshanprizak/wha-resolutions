The Third World Health Assembly, Having considered the provisions of Article 14 of the Constitution with regard to the selection of the country or region in which the next annual session shall be held, and the invitation extended by the United States of America to have the next session in that country, and After considering the extra costs involved and the additional demands on staff occasioned by having the Assembly away from headquarters, and particularly in view of the limitation on the funds available from the regular budget for 1951, 1. DECIDES that the Fourth World Health Assembly beheld at headquarters, and 2. EXPRESSES its genuine appreciation to the United States of America for its offer and its regret at being unable to accept this invitation, and further expresses the hope that at some future time it will be possible to hold an annual session in the United States of America. (Eighth report of the Committee on Administration, Finance and Legal Matters adopted at seventh plenary meeting, 25 May 1950) [A3 / R / 109]
